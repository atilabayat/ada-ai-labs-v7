Technical Analysis Trading Strategies

## **Strategy 1: Dual Moving Average Crossover (Momentum/Trend Following)**

Category: Momentum / Trend Following

Entry (Buy) Rules:

* Primary Trigger: The 50-period Simple Moving Average (SMA) crosses above the 200-period SMA (Golden Cross) on the daily chart   
* Confirmation: Price closes above both moving averages after the crossover  
* Context: Only take long signals when the broader trend is bullish (price above 200-period SMA)

Exit (Stoploss) Rules:

* Primary Exit: Sell when the 50-period SMA crosses below the 200-period SMA (Death Cross), signaling trend reversal   
* Stop-Loss: Place initial stop-loss 5-8% below entry price or at the most recent swing low  
* Trailing Stop: Once in profit by 10%, move stop-loss to break-even, then trail using the 50-period SMA as dynamic support

## **Strategy 2: RSI Oversold Bounce (Mean-Reversion)**

Category: Mean-Reversion

Entry (Buy) Rules:

* Trend Filter: Price must be above its 200-period SMA, confirming the long-term uptrend remains intact   
* Primary Trigger: 14-period RSI drops below 30 (oversold territory)   
* Confirmation: A bullish candlestick pattern (hammer, bullish engulfing) closes after the RSI crosses below 30  
* Divergence Enhancement: Stronger signal if price makes a lower low while RSI forms a higher low (bullish divergence) 

Exit (Stoploss) Rules:

* Profit Target: Exit when RSI rises above 70 (overbought) or at the recent swing high resistance level   
* Invalidation Exit: Exit immediately if RSI fails to recover above 35 within 5 trading days  
* Stop-Loss: Place stop-loss just below the recent swing low that triggered the oversold reading (typically 3-5% below entry)

## **Strategy 3: Bollinger Band Mean Reversion (Volatility-Based)**

Category: Mean-Reversion / Volatility

Entry (Buy) Rules:

* Primary Trigger: Price closes below the lower Bollinger Band (20-period, 2 standard deviations)   
* Extreme Condition: RSI also below 30 for confirmation of oversold extremity   
* Candlestick Confirmation: A bullish reversal candle (hammer, bullish engulfing) closes after touching the lower band  
* Context: Avoid entering if the broader market is in a confirmed downtrend (SPY below 200-period SMA)

Exit (Stoploss) Rules:

* Profit Target: Exit when price touches the middle Bollinger Band (20-period SMA) or the upper band   
* Stop-Loss: Place stop-loss 1 ATR below the lowest low of the reversal candle  
* Invalidation Exit: Exit if price closes below the lower band for three consecutive days without reversal

## **Strategy 4: Pullback to Moving Average with Trend Confirmation (Momentum/Mean-Reversion Hybrid)**

Category: Momentum / Pullback

Entry (Buy) Rules:

* Trend Filter: Price is above the 200-period SMA, and the 20-period SMA is above the 50-period SMA (bullish alignment)  
* Primary Trigger: Price pulls back to touch the 20-period or 50-period SMA during an uptrend   
* Entry Execution: Enter when a bullish candle closes after touching the moving average, showing rejection of lower prices  
* Volume Confirmation: Volume should contract during the pullback and expand on the entry candle

Exit (Stoploss) Rules:

* Profit Target: Set initial target at the recent swing high (typically 1.5x to 2x the risk amount)  
* Stop-Loss: Place stop-loss just below the moving average that triggered the entry (0.5-1 ATR below)  
* Trailing Exit: Once price moves 1 ATR in your favor, move stop-loss to break-even. Trail using the 20-period SMA  
* Time Exit: If price hasn't reached the target within 10-15 trading days, exit the position 

## **Strategy 5: Support/Resistance Breakout with Volume Confirmation (Breakout)**

Category: Breakout / Momentum

Entry (Buy) Rules:

* Key Level Identification: Identify a clear resistance level where price has rejected at least twice previously   
* Primary Trigger: Price closes above the identified resistance level with a strong bullish candle   
* Volume Confirmation: Volume on the breakout candle must be significantly above the 20-period average volume   
* ATR Context: The breakout move should exceed 1.5x the Average True Range (ATR) to confirm genuine momentum 

Exit (Stoploss) Rules:

* Profit Target 1 (Partial): Exit 50% of position at 1.5x ATR above breakout level  
* Profit Target 2 (Full): Exit remaining position at 3x ATR above breakout level or at next logical resistance level  
* Stop-Loss: Place initial stop-loss just below the breakout level (0.5-1 ATR below entry)   
* Trailing Stop: After hitting Target 1, move stop-loss to break-even. Consider using a trailing stop of 1 ATR  
* Invalidation Exit: Exit immediately if price falls back below the breakout level within 3 days (false breakout)

## **Strategy Comparison Summary**

| Strategy | Category | Best For | Typical Holding Period |
| :---- | :---- | :---- | :---- |
| Dual MA Crossover | Momentum/Trend | Trending markets | Weeks to months |
| RSI Oversold Bounce | Mean-Reversion | Range-bound/ranging markets | Days to 2 weeks |
| Bollinger Band Reversion | Mean-Reversion/Volatility | High volatility periods | Days to 1 week |
| Pullback to MA | Hybrid | Strong uptrends | 1-3 weeks |
| Support/Resistance Breakout | Breakout | Consolidation breakouts | Days to 2 weeks |

## **Practical Guidelines**

Risk Management for All Strategies:

* Never risk more than 1-2% of total account capital on any single trade   
* Maintain a minimum risk-reward ratio of 1:2 (risk $1 to make $2)   
* Use a trading journal to track performance and refine entry/exit rules 

Backtesting Recommendation:  
Before applying any strategy with real capital, test it using historical data or a demo account for at least 50-100 trades to validate its performance for your specific tickers
