Here **is a compiled** list **of** 100 popular **technical indicators commonly**

**used by professional stock chartists and day traders**.

Top day traders (the “best stock chartists”) rarely use dozens at once.

They typically rely on **price action \+** volume **\+ 3-7 core indicators**

tailored to their style (e.g., VWAP for intraday fair value, EMAs for trend,

RSI/MACD for momentum, Bollinger Bands for volatility). The list below

draws from comprehensive expert sources (including day-trading

guides, Quantified Strategies' collection of 100+ backtested indicators,

TradingView/StockCharts libraries, and professional references) and

focuses on those frequently cited for stocks and intraday trading. Many

are oscillators, overlays, or volume-based tools suited to short

timeframes (1-15 min charts).

I've grouped them loosely into categories for easier reference (with

some overlap, as many indicators serve multiple purposes). Variations

(e.g., different moving average types) are included because pros

customize periods and types heavily.

**Quick tips from pro** day **traders**:

·  
Core **setup for most chartists**: VWAP \+ 9/20/50 EMA \+ Volume \+

RSI (or Stochastic) \+ Bollinger Bands.

Always combine with pure **price action** (candlesticks,

support/resistance, breakouts).

• Test on your specific timeframe and assets — backtesting shows no

single indicator wins alone.

• Avoid "indicator overload"; the best chartists keep charts clean.
